# Level Balance — First 10 Levels

## Intent

The first ten levels are a soft introductory slice for a familiar genre. They
must demonstrate a scalable content model without forcing a long onboarding
sequence.

Players begin with three choices: easy, normal, and hard. They only need two
victories to continue, so a frustrating random board does not block the whole
campaign.

## Initial Balance Hypothesis

| Level | Difficulty | Moves | Collect target | 3 stars | 2 stars |
|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | Easy | 18 | 15 roses | 10+ left | 4+ left |
| 2 | Normal | 18 | 18 candles | 9+ | 4+ |
| 3 | Hard | 18 | 21 keys | 7+ | 3+ |
| 4 | Easy | 19 | 19 crystals | 10+ | 4+ |
| 5 | Normal | 19 | 23 bats | 9+ | 4+ |
| 6 | Hard | 19 | 27 scrolls | 7+ | 3+ |
| 7 | Easy | 20 | 22 roses | 10+ | 4+ |
| 8 | Normal | 20 | 27 candles | 8+ | 3+ |
| 9 | Hard | 20 | 32 keys | 6+ | 3+ |
| 10 | Finale | 22 | 34 crystals | 6+ | 2+ |

These numbers are hypotheses, not final production balance. Four-tile matches
and cascades are valid player skill/luck moments and should improve star results.

## Playtest Method

For each level, collect at least 10 attempts across multiple generated boards:

- win or loss;
- moves left;
- star result;
- number of attempts;
- unusually large matches or cascades;
- whether the player deliberately chose this difficulty or avoided it.

Tune one dimension at a time:

1. adjust collect target by 2-3;
2. adjust star thresholds;
3. adjust move limit only when win rate itself is wrong.

## Desired Early Experience

- easy choices should be highly reliable;
- normal choices should be the default path;
- hard choices should offer challenge without being mandatory;
- one-star wins always count toward group progression;
- three stars reward efficient play rather than being required to continue.
